What Drives the Work

Inspiration

The books, thinkers, projects, and principles that shape my approach to engineering and computation.

Books & Papers

"Dynamics of Structures"

Anil K. Chopra

The definitive text on hydrology and water resource systems. Every flood model I build has roots in this book.

"Deep Learning"

Goodfellow, Bengio & Courville

Mathematical rigor meeting neural network intuition. The bible for anyone serious about understanding deep learning from first principles.

"Physics-Informed Neural Networks"

Maziar Raissi et al.

The paper that crystallized my research direction — merging physics and deep learning for engineering applications.

Thinkers & Engineers

Asit K. Biswas

Water Resources Pioneer

Pioneer of integrated water resource management. His work on sustainable groundwater systems redefined how we approach water scarcity in developing nations.

Geoffrey Hinton

AI Research

Decades of persistence in neural network research when everyone else had given up. A reminder that breakthrough requires patience and conviction.

Andrej Karpathy

ML Education

Making deep learning intuitive and accessible. His teaching philosophy — build from scratch to truly understand — mirrors my engineering approach.

Guiding Principles

First Principles Thinking

Decompose problems to fundamental truths, rebuild from there. Never accept "that's how it's always been done" as an answer.

Occam's Razor in Engineering

The simplest model that explains the data is the most reliable. Complexity is the enemy of resilience in both code and concrete.

Build to Last, Not to Ship

Infrastructure thinking applied to software systems. Every system should be designed for 100 years of service, not just the next sprint.

Understand Before Optimizing

You cannot improve what you don't fully understand. Deep comprehension precedes meaningful optimization in any system.

"We shape our buildings; thereafter they shape us."

— Winston Churchill

This applies equally to the neural architectures we design. They shape the problems we can solve.
