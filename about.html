My foundation in civil engineering taught me how natural systems behave — how water flows through watersheds, how soil consolidates under load, how groundwater moves through aquifers.

This understanding of real-world physics informs every machine learning model I build. I don't treat data abstractly; I understand the environmental processes generating it, which leads to models with genuine predictive power.

Today, my focus lies at the intersection of AI-assisted environmental engineering, hydrology, geotechnical analysis, and water resource management. I develop deep learning architectures that can forecast floods, predict groundwater levels, and assess environmental impacts.
